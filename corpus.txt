The Eiffel Tower is located in Paris, France. It was completed in 1889 and is a famous landmark.
In 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to set foot on the moon during the Apollo 11 mission.
Albert Einstein, a theoretical physicist, developed the theory of relativity. He was born in Germany in 1879.
The Amazon Rainforest is home to a diverse range of plant and animal species. It is often referred to as the "lungs of the Earth" because of its crucial role in producing oxygen.
The Great Wall of China is a world-renowned fortification. It was built to protect against invasions and spans over 13,000 miles.
William Shakespeare, an English playwright, is widely regarded as one of the greatest writers in the English language. His works include plays like "Hamlet" and "Romeo and Juliet."
The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of eternal love.
Ada Lovelace is considered the world's first computer programmer. She worked on Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine in the 19th century.
The Serengeti National Park in Tanzania is famous for its annual migration of wildebeest and other animals.
Michael Jordan, a basketball legend, played the majority of his career with the Chicago Bulls. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time.
